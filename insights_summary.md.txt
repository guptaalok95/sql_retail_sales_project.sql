## 1. Executive Summary
This analysis evaluates retail sales data to uncover revenue drivers, customer behavior, profitability patterns, and operational trends.  
The goal is to support data-driven business decisions using SQL analytics.

---

## 2. Revenue & Growth Insights
- Repeat customers generate a significantly higher share of total revenue compared to one-time customers.
- Month-over-Month (MoM) analysis reveals seasonal spikes, indicating demand peaks during specific periods.
- A small percentage of customers contribute to nearly 80% of total revenue (Pareto Principle).

---

## 3. Customer Behavior Insights
- High-value customers (top NTILE segment) contribute the majority of profits.
- Customer churn analysis identified inactive customers who have not purchased in the last 3 months.
- Purchase frequency strongly correlates with higher Customer Lifetime Value (CLV).

---

## 4. Category & Product Performance
- Certain categories consistently outperform others in both revenue and profit margin.
- Profit margin ranking highlights categories that generate higher returns despite lower sales volume.
- Seasonal analysis shows category-specific demand variation between Q1 and Q4.

---

## 5. Time-Based & Operational Insights
- Evening hours generate the highest sales volume, indicating optimal timing for promotions.
- Peak sales hours help identify staffing and inventory optimization opportunities.
- Sales volatility analysis shows higher fluctuations during festive months.

---

## 6. Profitability Insights
- Categories with higher sales do not always yield higher profit margins.
- Cost analysis reveals opportunities to optimize COGS in low-margin categories.

---

## 7. Business Recommendations
- Focus retention strategies on high-CLV and repeat customers.
- Allocate marketing budgets during peak sales hours and high-growth months.
- Optimize inventory for top-performing categories and seasonal demand.
- Re-engage churned customers using targeted offers.

---

## 8. Conclusion
This project demonstrates how advanced SQL techniques such as window functions, CTEs, ranking, and time-series analysis can deliver actionable business insights from transactional retail data.
